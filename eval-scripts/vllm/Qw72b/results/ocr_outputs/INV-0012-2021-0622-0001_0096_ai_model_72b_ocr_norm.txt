(ii) to improve the functioning of the community's institutions by making some adjustments to the balance of responsibilities between the commission, the european parliament, the european council, and the european court of justice. it was also intended to develop better mechanisms for bringing together the political and economic arms of the community; and to clarify the use of majority voting and of the luxembourg compromise. progress has been made, especially as regards widening the scope of political co-operation, but major disagreements have emerged in discussions of institutional matters. this must mean that there can be little prospect of an early adoption of an act along the lines proposed by ministers genscher and colombo. but as in the past, i have no doubt that the logic of events will continue to carry the community forward. in recent months, we have seen renewed proof that the community has a vitality which carries it, when necessary, beyond the neat distinctions so beloved of our member states' experts. the tripartite agreement between the council, the commission and .../3
