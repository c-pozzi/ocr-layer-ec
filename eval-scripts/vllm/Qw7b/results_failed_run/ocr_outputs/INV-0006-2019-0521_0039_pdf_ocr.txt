4· Kitzbühel — Plöcken-Pass (Monte Croce Cárnico)
This road provides a link between Germany and North-East Italy, 
notably the region of Friuli - Venezia Giulia and the ports of 
Trieste and Venice. A motorway links Trieste and Venice to Tolmezzo, 
35 KM South of the Austrian border. From there the shortest route to 
Germany is via the high pass of Monte Croce Cárnico. 
■»
Planned improvements to the road and a project for a tunnel 
under the pass were included in the Italian contribution to the 
bottleneck report. The Italian and Austrian Governments have both 
pronounced in favour of the construction of a tunnel and negotiations 
for a treaty are under way. The tunnel is regarded as particularly 
important for the development of Friuli-Venezia Giulia. The possibility 
of aid from the Regional Development Fund is under discussion.
Although the Plöcken-Pass is situated in Carinthia, the shortest 
route to Germany passes through the East Tirol, Salzburgerland and 
North Tirol. The only works planned along this route, as far as we 
know, are at its extreme northern end: the construction of new
