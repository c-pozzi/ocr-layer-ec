There has been quite good progress in developing Community policies and in
introducing a number of reforms to the Agricultural Policy. But it has not
been possible to do more than extend for a further year the ad hoc budgetary
mechanism previously adopted in 1980. The Commission is therefore called upon
to make new proposals for a pluriannual budgetary mechanism, with a view to
a Council decision by November this year.

Despite difficult discussions, the Commission managed to avoid there being
any blockage in the Community's decision-making during the critical month
of May. This gives grounds for confidence that the necessary degree of
serenity can be maintained this Autumn also.

The European Act, proposed by ministers GENSCHER and COLOMBO, was designed :

(i) to widen the scope of Europe's political co-operation, to include
in particular some political and economic aspects of security policy,
and to strengthen co-operation in a number of areas of jurisprudence
outside the scope of the existing Treaties.

(ii) to improve the functioning of the Community's institutions by
making some adjustments to the balance of responsibilities
between the Commission, the European Parliament, the European
Council, and the European Court of Justice. It was also intended
to develop better mechanisms for bringing together the political
and economic arms of the Community; and to clarify the use of
majority voting and of the Luxembourg compromise.

Progress has been made, especially as regards widening the scope of political
co-operation, but major disagreements have emerged in discussions of insti-
tutional matters. This must mean that there can be little prospect of an
early adoption of an Act along the lines proposed by ministers GENSCHER
and COLOMBO. But as in the past, I have no doubt that the logic of events
